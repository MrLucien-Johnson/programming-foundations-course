# Pricing Strategy: Programming Foundations Course

## 🎯 Pricing Philosophy

**Our approach:** Fair, accessible, and value-focused. We want to make quality programming education available to beginners and career changers while ensuring the course is sustainable.

**Key principles:**
- Price reflects real value, not inflated promises
- Accessible to target audience (beginners/career changers)
- Bundle provides clear value advantage
- Early buyers rewarded for taking a chance
- Prices can grow as course proves value

## 🎯 Anchor Pricing Strategy

### The Anchor: Bundle at $129 (Future Full Price)

**Why:** The bundle represents the "complete" experience. By showing this as the full price, the launch price of $79 feels like significant value.

**Psychology:**
- Bundle is the "premium" option
- Single courses feel accessible at $49
- Bundle discount feels substantial
- Creates clear value hierarchy

### Launch Pricing (Early Buyer Reward)

**Duration:** First 30-60 days OR first 50-100 buyers (whichever comes first)

**Rationale:**
- Rewards early adopters for taking a chance
- Generates initial revenue and feedback
- Builds social proof through early buyers
- Creates natural urgency without fake scarcity

### When to Raise Prices

✅ **Raise prices when:**
- You have 20+ satisfied buyers
- You've received positive feedback
- You've made improvements based on feedback
- You have testimonials/social proof
- Course has proven value

❌ **Don't raise prices when:**
- You have fewer than 10 buyers
- You haven't received feedback yet
- Course hasn't been improved
- No social proof exists
- You're feeling uncertain

## 💡 Pricing Psychology Tips

### What Works

✅ **Clear value hierarchy** - Bundle clearly best value
✅ **Early buyer pricing** - Rewards risk-takers
✅ **Lifetime access** - Reduces buyer hesitation
✅ **Portfolio focus** - Tangible outcome
✅ **Two languages** - Clear differentiation

### What to Avoid

❌ **Fake urgency** - "Only 24 hours left!" (unless true)
❌ **Over-discounting** - Devalues the course
❌ **Complex pricing** - Keep it simple
❌ **Price anchoring too high** - Unrealistic full price
❌ **Constant sales** - Erodes trust

## 📋 Pricing Decision Framework

### When Setting Prices, Consider:

1. **Target audience income level** - Career changers may have limited budgets
2. **Competitive landscape** - What are alternatives charging?
3. **Value delivered** - What are buyers actually getting?
4. **Your costs** - Time invested, platform fees (if any)
5. **Market validation** - What will people actually pay?

### Price Testing Approach

- **Start with launch prices** - Test market response
- **Gather feedback** - What do buyers think about value?
- **Adjust gradually** - Small increases, not dramatic jumps
- **Monitor conversion** - Are people buying at this price?
- **Stay flexible** - Pricing isn't set in stone
